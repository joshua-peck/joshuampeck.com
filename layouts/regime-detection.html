The Three Regimes

The model produces one of three classifications. Each regime has a distinct statistical character — not in average returns, but in the distribution of risk around those returns.

{{/* Regime cards — three column */}}
{{/* BOOM */}}
Boom

Broad market participation, trending prices, compressing volatility, positive momentum across sectors.

21-day Sharpe 1.34
Mean return / 21d +1.8%
Observations 1,142

The environment in which equity risk is best compensated. Not a signal to lever up — a signal that the wind is at your back.

{{/* MID-MARKET */}}
Mid-Market

No acute stress signals. No strong directional momentum. Markets are moving without a clear behavioral thesis in either direction.

21-day Sharpe 0.49
Mean return / 21d +0.5%
Observations 2,336

The default state. Markets spend more time here than anywhere else. The absence of a strong signal is itself information.

{{/* DANGER */}}
Danger

Credit spreads widening, volatility expanding, momentum deteriorating, macro indicators signaling stress.

21-day Sharpe 0.30
Mean return / 21d +0.5%
Observations 1,508

Same average return as Mid-Market. Twice the downside risk. The mean hides the distribution — Danger periods produce the left tail events that define drawdowns.

{{/* Key insight connector */}}

The mean return in Danger is nearly identical to Mid-Market. The Sharpe ratio is not. That gap — 0.30 versus 0.49 — is the entire argument for regime awareness.

The equity curve applies a simple position-sizing rule to the regime classifications: reduce exposure in Danger, maintain full exposure in Mid-Market and near-full in Boom. No forecasting. No price targets. The only input is the current regime.

The primary result is not a higher-returning strategy — though the CAGR improvement from 8.4% to 11.2% is meaningful. The primary result is a different risk profile: lower volatility, lower maximum drawdown, better Sharpe. The regime-weighted curve doesn't beat the market by taking more risk. It produces better risk-adjusted returns by taking less at the wrong times.
